Storage Architecture

Only New Decisions.
Only New Observations.

PLATO's delta recording doesn't store full snapshots. It stores what changed. A full voyage log might be 80KB. The delta — what actually happened that's new — is 400 bytes. Your storage budget drops by 95-99%. Your intelligence doesn't.

When you file a decision or log an observation, PLATO records only the delta. When you query, it reconstructs the complete picture from deltas across your entire history. Accuracy stays at 100%. Storage compounds at near-zero cost.

Operation Flow

From Orders to Compounded Intelligence

Three actions. One room. Intelligence that compounds every season.

1

File Departure Orders

Enter your captain's orders — destination, crew assignments, expected conditions. Capitaine tiles this into the voyage room as the delta baseline. The room now knows what you planned.

2

Log en Route, File Observations

Waypoints reached. Conditions changed. Crew decisions made. Every update is a tile — small, specific, delta-only. The room accumulates what actually happened, not what you planned.

3

Query Your Intelligence

Ask anything across your accumulated history. "What worked on the south grounds in August last year?" "Which crew assignments gave the best efficiency?" PLATO reconstructs from deltas, answers from experience.
